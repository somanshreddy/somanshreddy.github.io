Academia or Industry?

I would like to work in the industry. However, I have noticed that many complex problems in the industry are often solved by research. I would like to pursue a more systematic and rigorous approach to research to help me in the industry. Hence, I plan to apply for a Master's in CS with a concentration in Systems and Machine Learning. This would also help me answer the above questions that I am interested in.

Masters or Ph.D.?

Initially, I had not even considered a Ph.D. due to the sheer length of the program. It did not make sense to study for 6+ years when my future goal was to work in the industry. However, it was only during my final year of my undergraduate that I started to understand the intent behind a Ph.D.

I have read many interesting papers this year which involve Systems + X, where X is any other field. Among the papers I enjoyed reading, the X was usually Machine Learning, or the Cloud, or even both. I'll admit that there have been a few instances where I was amazed and stopped to think, "I really want to work on this! Maybe I should consider a Ph.D. after my Masters..."

However, right now I believe that a Master's would give me sufficient exposure to research in this field before I join the industry. I am currently reading research published at top Systems conferences so that I will be able to start contributing early to research labs when I join graduate school.
